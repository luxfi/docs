Every app you use today works the same way: your data lives on someone else's computer, encrypted with someone else's keys, governed by someone else's terms. You rent access to your own information.

We built the replacement.

## The Thesis

**Base Vault SDK is the personal computer of Web5. Lux is the trust kernel.**

- **Base** owns state — encrypted SQLite vault, local-first, offline-capable
- **Lux** owns trust — identity, key policy, threshold governance, receipts
- **Providers** own service quality — relay, storage, compute, bandwidth
- **Users** own keys and exit rights — switch providers without losing data

## Why Old Crypto Failed

The first generation tried to put everything on-chain. Users got 30-second confirmations, \$50 gas, and public state anyone could read.

Chain is not a database. Chain is **trust infrastructure** — the place you go when two parties need agreement they can't provide alone.

## The Capsule

Every user, org, and AI agent gets a capsule: encrypted vault, CRDT sync, DID identity, threshold controls, audit receipts, and compute policy. Cloud is optional replication. Chain is optional trust anchoring.

## Three Classes of Apps

**Local-first private.** Install an app, get a vault. Encrypted, local, yours.

**Threshold.** Important actions require quorum. Admin isn't a password — it's 2-of-3 attesting.

**Confidential compute.** Private AI inference, sealed-bid auctions, encrypted analytics. FHE creates qualitatively new products.

## The Stack

**Default:** AES-256-GCM + ML-KEM. Fast, post-quantum, every secret at rest.

**Threshold:** TFHE gates reveal. Validators attest before decrypt.

**Confidential compute:** CKKS for encrypted ML. GPU-accelerated, 40x over CPU.

## 15 Chains, One Validator

Lux runs 15 specialized chains on every validator: I-Chain (identity), K-Chain (keys), T-Chain (threshold FHE), M-Chain (MPC signing), plus EVM, DEX, FHE, ZK, bridge, quantum consensus, AI inference, oracles, graph, relay, and Beluga L3.

No separate deployments. Validators stake and do everything.

## AI Agents as Sovereign Processes

Every agent gets its own capsule: encrypted vault for persistent memory, DID identity, scoped capabilities, threshold spend controls, private inference via CKKS.

Agents stop being chatbot wrappers and start being sovereign processes.
